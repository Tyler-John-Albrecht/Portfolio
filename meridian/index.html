Asked before reserving

Quiet answers to fair questions.

Why one car per bay per day?
Because rushing is how swirl marks happen. A booked day means your car is never moved aside for a walk-in, never polished against a clock, and never handed to whoever's free. One craftsman starts it and finishes it.
Is ceramic coating actually worth it?
On corrected paint, yes — washing gets dramatically easier and UV, contaminants, and water spots stop bonding to the surface. On uncorrected paint, no — which is why we never apply ceramic over defects, even when asked.
How far in advance should I book?
Maintenance details usually book within the week. Corrections and Atelier Days run two to three weeks out. Members skip the queue entirely — that's most of what the membership is.
Do you work on daily drivers, or just exotics?
Both. The standard doesn't change with the badge — a well-kept daily gets the same light, the same process, and the same walkaround as a Chiron.
