Before you book

Fair questions, straight answers

Ceramic coating vs. PPF — which do I actually need?
Different jobs. Ceramic makes the car easier to keep clean and blocks UV; PPF physically absorbs rock chips and scratches. High-impact panels (front bumper, hood) want PPF; the rest of the car wants ceramic. Most of our packages combine both for exactly that reason.
How long does installation take?
Tint is same-day. Ceramic packages run 1–2 days depending on paint condition. Full-front PPF is 2–3 days because film edges are wrapped, not trimmed on your paint. You get a firm timeline with your quote.
Will PPF yellow or peel like the old stuff?
Modern film is a different animal — self-healing topcoats, 10-year manufacturer warranties, and no yellowing. The failures you've seen on older cars are either aged film or bad installation. We fix one with chemistry and the other with craft.
Which location should I book?
Whichever is closer — Lakeland and Riverview run the same equipment, same training, same standard. Your warranty and service history work at both.
