Before you ask

Tint questions, straight answers

Is dark tint legal in Florida?
Front side windows must allow 28%+ of light through; rear sides and back glass can go to 15%. We know the rules cold and will keep your car street-legal — or walk you through the medical exemption process if you qualify.
How long does installation take?
A full sedan takes about two hours; SUVs and trucks slightly longer. Windshield strips and two-front matches are usually under an hour. You'll get a firm time when you book.
When can I roll my windows down?
Give the film 3–5 days to cure before rolling windows down. The haze and tiny water pockets you might see in the first week are normal — they evaporate as the film sets.
Will tint mess with my defroster or radio?
No. Our films install over defroster lines without affecting them, and unlike old metallic films, ceramic and carbon films don't interfere with GPS, cell, toll tags, or keyless entry.
What does the warranty actually cover?
Bubbling, peeling, cracking, and color change — covered for the life of the film on your vehicle, parts and labor. If something's wrong, we fix the panel. Done.
